Yantra Definition

तत्र मनःशरीराबाधकराणि शल्यानी, तेषामहरणाभ्युपायो यन्त्राणि ||

Clinical Manifestation: Any structural apparatus utilized as a systematic solution framework to extract internal or extraneous foreign elements (Shalya) causing trauma to the psychosome (Manas & Sharira) is called a Yantra.

Yantra Types (Sankhya System)

Acharya Sushruth (101 Total)

  1. Swastik Yantra (Cruciform Instruments / 24)
  2. Sandansh Yantra (Pincer-like Instruments / 2)
  3. Tala Yantra (Spoon-shaped Instruments / 2)
  4. Nadi Yantra (Tubular Diagnostic Instruments / 20)
  5. Shalaka Yantra (Rod-like Instruments / Probes / 28)
  6. Upayantra (Accessory/Improvised Tools / 25)

According to Ashtang Sangraha

  1. Swastik Yantra 2) Sandansh Yantra 3) Taal Yantra 4) Naadi Yantra 5) Shalaka Yantra 6) Anuyantra

According to Harit Samhita

  1. Godhamukha 2) Vajramukha 3) Sandansh 4) Chakrakruti 5) Kankapaad 6) Aanaka 7) Shrungak 8) Kundal 9) Shrivatsa 10) Sauvatsik 11) Panchavaktra 12) Grudhrapaad

Yantra Vishay (Indications)

  • Avagadha Vrana: Deep, multi-layered complex wounds.
  • Anumukha Vrana: Narrow sinus tracks and small wound openings.
  • Shalyayukta Vrana: Complex wound scenarios with embedded foreign bodies.

Yantra Nirman Dravya (Materials)

  • Loha: Iron/Steel
  • Suvarna: Gold
  • Rajat: Silver
  • Tamra: Copper
  • Shrunga: Horn
  • Gajadanta: Ivory/Tusk

Yantra Akriti (Shapes)

Yantra are mainly modeled according to the shape of the mouth of various violent and aggressive animals, birds, and deer.

Yantra Guna (Qualities)

समाहितानि यन्त्राणि खरश्लक्ष्णमुखानि च | सुरूपाणि सुदृढाणि सुग्रहाणि च कारयेत् ||
  1. Samahitani (Equally aligned) 2) Khara mukha (Furrowed) 3) Shlakshna Mukha (Smooth) 4) Surupa (Well-shaped) 5) Sudrudha (Highly robust) 6) Sugraha (Easy to hold)

Yantra Dosha (Mechanical Structural Flaws)

  • 1) Atisthula: Too thick, causing tissue trauma.
  • 2) Asara: Lacks structural strength (weak, soft).
  • 3) Ati-dirgha: Too long. 4) Ati-hrasva: Too short.
  • 5) Agrahi: Fails to hold object firmly. 6) Vishama-grahi: Uneven, skewed hold.
  • 7) Vakra: Crooked or asymmetrical. 8) Shithila: Loose in joints.
  • 9) Atyunnata: Unnecessary bulge disruption. 10) Mrdukila: Soft core pin/rivet.
  • 11) Mrdu-mukha: Soft active working tips. 12) Mrdu-pasa: Loose loops/ties.
  • 1) Ati Dirgha: Too long. 2) Ati Hrisva: Too short.
  • 3) Sthula: Too thick. 4) Tanu: Thin / fragile.
  • 5) Vakra: Crooked/bent. 6) Vishamagrahi: Improper skewed grip.
  • 7) Agrahi: Not holding well. 8) Shithila: Loose pivot or weak body.

Prashastra Yantra

Yantra without any mentioned doshas, measuring 18 Angula, with all mentioned yantra Gunas (Qualities) is called Prashastra Yantra. Kankamukh yantra is considered the foremost Prashastra yantra.

Yantra Kosh (Instrument Preservation Unit)

Definition: Yantra Kosh is a special box container used for storing and protecting surgical instruments (Yantras and Shastras).

According to Sushruta, the instrument box should be strong, clean, well-arranged, and capable of protecting instruments from damage, rust, dust, and contamination.

Dimensional Layout Framework: The Shastra Kosh parameters measure exactly 9 Angula wide and 12 Angula long.
